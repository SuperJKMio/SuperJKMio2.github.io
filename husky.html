The Goal

HuskyYum! is a mobile application that aims to help health-conscious students quickly and easily access ways to create and find healthy meals around campus.

The HuskyYum! mobile application lets users search, post and rate recipes. Users can use the recipes to create their own personalized shopping lists based on the recipes that they are interested in making. Students can also use HuskyYum! to look for healthy meals around campus when they do not have the time to prepare a healthy homemade meal.

Research

We used the methods of suvery, interview and fly on the wall to understand the potential users for our project, UW students. Our research questions were:

• Who will benefit the most with greater access to healthy food options?
• What kind of food accessibility issues are students currently facing?
• What kind of food options are currently available for students?
• When do students seek out food options?
• Where are food options most readily available to students?
• Why is eating healthy food a challenge for students?

The survey was shared on Facebook and distributed via various UW mailing lists for 7 days. 37 responses were collected.

We conducted extensive observations of students during lunchtime. In particular, we paid attention to individual student’s behaviors, group dynamics, and students’ comments about food.

Our team conducted a number of Interviews with stakeholders, including 13 interviews with UW students and one interview with a food vendor.

Affinity Diagram

We chose affinity diagram to organize and visualize our ideas and facts. The affinity diagram reveals 5 groups of major characters of our target users: Time, Attitudes toward food, Social, Lifestyle, Uncertainties.

Persona

According to our research findings, we identified the main user needs:

• Peer influence can be a great motivation for students.
• Cannot compromise dynamic lifestyle and fast-paced schedule.
• Students who want to adopt healthier eating habits benefit from sharing peers’ experience.

We created three personas reflecting the three main groups of customers we targeted at: health conscious student, confused graduate student, and peanut-free undergraduate studet.

Ideation

We brainstormed and came up with several ideas for both functions and work flow of the app. We sketched our ideas and grouped different designs of the same function togehter.

We created three personas reflecting the three main groups of customers we targeted at: health conscious student, confused graduate student, and peanut-free undergraduate studet.

Prototyping

We had 4 rounds of iteration in lo-fi prototyping and 3 rounds of usability tests in between. After each usability test, we rapidly update the prototype to solve the usability issues identified.

Evaluation

We designed and conducted usability tests on our prototype. The usability tests of HuskyYum! focused on the user’s ability to search, post and to utilize the extra product features. The usability test tasks were designed to assess the accessibility and understandability of the beginning-to-end user flow for the major product features of the mobile application.

User tests on prototype

Outcome

We created an interactive hi-fi prototype in Justinmind and came up with a design specification to descripe the design of our entire project, which served as a blueprint for a developer who would be charged with the actual implementation of our design.
